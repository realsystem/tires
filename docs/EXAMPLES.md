## Example 1: 2020 Toyota Tacoma TRD Off-Road

### Stock Configuration
- **Tires**: 265/70R16
- **Wheels**: 16x7 +30mm offset
- **Gear Ratio**: 3.73
- **Suspension**: Stock IFS
- **Transmission**: 6MT (final gear 1.0)
- **Transfer Case**: 2.56:1 low range
- **First Gear**: 4.03:1

### Proposed Upgrade: 285/75R16 (33" tire)

**Calculator Input**:
```
Current: 265/70R16
New: 285/75R16
Axle Ratio: 3.73
Transmission Top: 1.0
Transfer Low: 2.56
First Gear: 4.03
Use: Weekend Trail
Suspension: IFS
```

**Results**:

| Metric | Stock | New | Change |
|--------|-------|-----|--------|
| Diameter | 30.6" | 32.8" | +2.2" (+7.2%) |
| Width | 10.4" | 11.2" | +0.8" (+7.5%) |
| Ground Clearance | - | - | +1.1" gain |
| Circumference | 96.1" | 103.1" | +7.0" |

**Speedometer Error**:
- 30 mph indicated = 32.2 mph actual (+2.2 mph)
- 60 mph indicated = 64.3 mph actual (+4.3 mph)
- 75 mph indicated = 80.4 mph actual (+5.4 mph)

**Drivetrain Impact**:
- Original RPM @ 65 mph: 2,145 RPM
- New RPM @ 65 mph: 2,000 RPM (-145 RPM)
- Effective gear ratio: 3.48 (vs 3.73 stock)
- Stock crawl ratio: 38.5:1
- New crawl ratio: 35.9:1 (-6.8%)

**Recommendations**:

**Re-Gearing**: CONSIDER
- Diameter change 7.2% is moderate
- Performance loss noticeable but acceptable
- Re-gear to 4.10 would restore most performance
- Alternative: 4.27 for improved crawl ratio

**Modifications Required**:
1. **Suspension**: 2" lift recommended
   - Bilstein 5100s or similar
   - OME medium/heavy springs
   - Extended brake lines
   - Diff drop or UCA upgrade

2. **Clearance**:
   - Minor fender liner trimming
   - Pinch weld modification likely
   - Body mount chop (BMC) may be needed at full stuff

3. **Wheels**:
   - 0mm to +20mm offset recommended
   - Stock +30mm may work but tight
   - 1.25" spacers as alternative

**Warnings**:
- ‚ö†Ô∏è IFS CV angles will increase - monitor for clicking/binding
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Fuel economy will drop 1-2 MPG
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Slight power loss in 6th gear on highway hills

**Verdict**: Solid upgrade for weekend wheeling. Stays within "bolt-on" modification range. No re-gear required but recommended for best performance.

## Example 1B: 2023 Toyota Tacoma TRD Off-Road (Auto w/ 3.909 Gears)

### Stock Configuration
- **Tires**: 265/70R17
- **Wheels**: 17x7.5 TRD alloy
- **Gear Ratio**: 3.909 (factory automatic transmission option)
- **Suspension**: TRD Off-Road suspension (Bilstein shocks)
- **Transmission**: 6-speed Auto (0.84 OD)
- **Transfer Case**: 2.566:1 low range
- **First Gear**: 3.53:1

### Proposed Upgrade: 285/75R17 (33" tire)

**Calculator Input**:
```
Current: 265/70R17
New: 285/75R17
Axle Ratio: 3.909
Transmission Top: 0.84
Transfer Low: 2.566
First Gear: 3.53
Use: Weekend Trail
Suspension: IFS
```

**Results**:

| Metric | Stock | New | Change |
|--------|-------|-----|--------|
| Diameter | 31.6" | 32.8" | +1.2" (+3.8%) |
| Width | 10.4" | 11.2" | +0.8" (+7.5%) |
| Ground Clearance | - | - | +0.6" gain |
| Circumference | 99.3" | 103.1" | +3.8" |

**Speedometer Error**:
- 30 mph indicated = 31.1 mph actual (+1.1 mph)
- 60 mph indicated = 62.3 mph actual (+2.3 mph)
- 75 mph indicated = 77.8 mph actual (+2.8 mph)

**Drivetrain Impact**:
- Original RPM @ 65 mph: 1,959 RPM
- New RPM @ 65 mph: 1,888 RPM (-71 RPM)
- Effective gear ratio: 3.77 (vs 3.909 stock)
- Stock crawl ratio: 35.4:1
- New crawl ratio: 34.1:1 (-3.8%)

**Recommendations**:

**Re-Gearing**: OPTIONAL
- The 3.909 gear ratio handles this tire size well
- Only 3.8% diameter increase is minimal
- RPM drop is minor (-71 RPM)
- Performance impact is barely noticeable

**Suggested Gear Ratio** (if you want to restore performance):
- **4.10**: Restores close to stock RPM
  - RPM @ 65 mph: 1,980 (near stock)
  - Crawl ratio: 36.4:1
  - Cost: $1,500-$2,200 installed

- **4.30**: Slight improvement over stock
  - RPM @ 65 mph: 2,077
  - Crawl ratio: 38.2:1
  - Better for loaded overland rigs

**Verdict**: Best option is to run the 285/75R17 on stock 3.909 gears. The factory ratio is already fairly steep, so the minor tire increase doesn't hurt much.

**Modifications Required**:

1. **Suspension**: 1.5-2" lift sufficient
   - Bilstein 5100s front (set to 1.75")
   - AAL or Dakar leaf pack rear
   - Total lift: ~2"

2. **Clearance**:
   - Minor fender liner trimming
   - Pinch weld fold or trim
   - May rub slightly at full lock when flexed

3. **Wheels**:
   - Stock TRD wheels work
   - Or upgrade to 0 to +10mm offset for better clearance
   - No spacers required

**Why 3.909 Gears are Great**:
- Middle ground between 3.73 (economy) and 4.10 (performance)
- Better highway RPM than 4.10 (fuel economy)
- Stronger acceleration than 3.73 (loaded performance)
- Ideal for overlanding with gear + roof tent

**Air-Down Guide**:
- Street: 35 PSI
- Dirt roads: 30 PSI
- Trail: 22-26 PSI
- Rock crawling: 15-18 PSI (monitor sidewall flex)

**Warnings**:
- ‚ö†Ô∏è IFS CV angles will increase slightly - monitor for clicking
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Fuel economy may drop 0.5-1 MPG (minimal due to low diameter increase)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Speedometer will read 2.3 mph slow at 60 mph (minor, but note for tickets)

**Real-World Experience**:
This is one of the best "stealth" upgrades for a Tacoma TRD. The 3.909 gears are already fairly aggressive, so the 285/75R17 tire sits perfectly in the sweet spot. You get:
- Improved ground clearance
- Better tire footprint off-road
- Minimal performance loss
- No re-gear needed
- Fits with moderate lift

**Total Cost**:
- Tires (5 with spare): $1,500 - $1,800
- Lift kit (2"): $600 - $900
- Wheels (optional): $800 - $1,200
- Installation/alignment: $300 - $500
- **Total: $2,400 - $3,200** (without wheels)
- **Total: $3,200 - $4,400** (with wheels)

**Perfect for**: Daily driver that sees weekend trails, light overlanding, forest service roads, occasional rock crawling.

**Verdict**: The 3.909 gear ratio is a hidden gem. Combined with 285/75R17 tires, you get a capable off-road setup without the need for expensive re-gearing. This is the most cost-effective Tacoma upgrade.

```
Current: 33x10.50R17

```

**Results**:

| Metric | Stock (33") | New (37") | Change |
|--------|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Diameter | 33.0" | 37.0" | +4.0" (+12.1%) |
| Width | 10.5" | 12.5" | +2.0" (+19.0%) |

| Circumference | 103.7" | 116.2" | +12.6" |

**Speedometer Error**:

- Crawl ratio: 54.5:1 (excellent for technical rock)
- Restores +98.5% of stock performance
- Highway RPM: comfortable, not buzzy

   - Track bar relocation
   - Adjustable control arms
   - Longer shocks
   - Extended brake lines

2. **Clearance**:
   - Flat fenders or high-clearance fenders
   - Pinch weld removal
   - Rocker panel protection
   - Bumper trimming possible

3. **Other**:
   - Wheels: 4.5" backspacing or less
   - Heavy-duty tie rod and drag link
   - Consider steering box brace
   - Upgrade ball joints

**Warnings**:
- üî¥ CRITICAL: Do not run 37s on 4.10 gears for extended periods
- üî¥ Transmission overheating likely without re-gear
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Brake upgrade strongly recommended (larger rotors, pads)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Fuel economy: expect 12-14 MPG (from 17-18 stock)

**Air-Down Guide**:
- Street: 32 PSI
- Trail: 18-22 PSI
- Rock: 8-12 PSI (beadlocks essential)

**Total Cost Estimate**:
- Tires (5 with spare): $2,500 - $3,000
- Wheels (5 beadlocks): $2,000 - $3,500
- Suspension lift: $2,500 - $4,500
- Re-gearing (front + rear): $2,400 - $3,200
- Installation/alignment: $500 - $800
- **Total: $10,000 - $15,000**

**Verdict**: This is a serious build. Not for casual weekend warriors. Requires commitment to both budget and off-road capability. Results in a highly capable rock crawler but compromises street manners. Re-gearing is non-negotiable.

**Speedometer Error**:
- Minimal: 60 mph indicated = 62.1 mph actual

1. **Suspension**: TRD Pro suspension adequate
   - May add 0.5" spacer for extra clearance
   - Consider heavy-duty coils if running roof rack + RTT

2. **Clearance**: Minimal work
   - Trim inner fender liner slightly
   - May rub at full lock when flexed

3. **Wheels**:
   - Stock TRD Pro wheels work
   - Or upgrade to 0 offset for better clearance

**Overlanding Considerations**:

**Loaded Weight**:
- Rig + gear + 2 adults + roof tent: ~6,800 lbs
- LT285/70R17 Load Range E: 3,640 lbs per tire
- 4 tires: 14,560 lbs capacity ‚úì Adequate

- Stock fuel economy: 18 MPG highway
- New fuel economy: ~17 MPG highway (-1 MPG)
- Tank: 23 gallons
- Range: ~390 miles highway, ~310 mixed terrain
- **Recommendation**: Add 5-gallon jerry can for remote areas

**Air Pressure Strategy**:
- Highway loaded: 38 PSI (heavier than stock due to RTT)
- Dirt roads: 32 PSI
- Mild trail: 26-28 PSI

- OEM spare location fits 285/70R17 with minor mod

**Total Cost**:
- Tires (5): $1,500 - $2,000
- No re-gear needed: $0
- Minor suspension: $200 - $500
- **Total: $1,700 - $2,500**

**Verdict**: Ideal overland upgrade. Maintains highway comfort, improves clearance, doesn't break the bank. The 4.30 gears are perfect for this tire size. Best bang-for-buck on a 5th gen 4Runner.

## Example 4: Budget Build - 2005 Tacoma PreRunner

### Stock Configuration
- **Tires**: 265/65R17 (street tire)
- **Wheels**: 17" alloys
- **Gear Ratio**: 3.73 (2WD)
- **Suspension**: 2WD PreRunner (leaf rear)
- **Transmission**: 5-speed manual (1.0 final)
- **No Transfer Case** (2WD)

### Proposed Upgrade: 285/70R17 (budget AT tire)

**Calculator Input**:
```
Current: 265/65R17
New: 285/70R17
Axle Ratio: 3.73
Transmission Top: 1.0
Use: Daily Driver + Light Trail
Suspension: IFS (2WD)
```

**Results**:

| Metric | Stock | New | Change |
|--------|-------|-----|--------|
| Diameter | 30.6" | 32.7" | +2.1" (+6.9%) |

**Drivetrain Impact**:
- Original RPM @ 65 mph: 2,189 RPM
- New RPM @ 65 mph: 2,048 RPM (-141 RPM)

**Re-Gearing**: OPTIONAL (budget constraint)

**Budget Build Plan**:

**Phase 1: Tires Only ($800)**
- 285/70R17 budget AT (Falken Wildpeak AT3W or similar)
- Run on stock wheels with 1.25" spacers
- Stock suspension
- Minor rubbing acceptable

**Phase 2: Minimal Lift ($500)**
- Bilstein 5100s front (set to 2")
- Add-a-leaf or Dakar leaf pack rear
- Extended bump stops
- Total lift: ~2.5"

**Phase 3: Wheels + Clearancing ($600)**
- Method or Level 8 wheels (0 offset)
- Trim fender liner
- BMC (body mount chop)
- Remove spacers

**Phase 4: Re-gear (Future - $1,500)**
- 4.10 or 4.27 gears (when budget allows)
- Restores performance
- Not critical for 2WD daily driver

**Warnings**:
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Power loss noticeable in 5th gear hills
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Not ideal for highway towing
- ‚úì Acceptable for daily driving + light trails

**Verdict**: Reasonable budget upgrade. You'll feel the power loss, but 2WD means less stress than 4WD. Save up for re-gear later if needed.

**Common Tacoma Gear Ratios**:
- **3.73**: Early models, manual transmission, economy focus
- **3.909**: 2016-2023 TRD Off-Road Auto (3rd gen), 2024+ with certain packages (best all-around)

- **4.30**: 4Runner TRD Pro, ideal for overlanding
- **4.56+**: Rock crawling builds, lifted with 35"+ tires

**Why 3.909 is Special**:
The 3.909 ratio is Toyota's "sweet spot" for the Tacoma TRD Off-Road with automatic transmission. It provides:
- Better acceleration than 3.73 (especially loaded)
- Better fuel economy than 4.10 (highway cruising)
- Handles 285/75R17 (33") tires without re-gearing
- Ideal for overlanding with gear and roof tent
- Found in 2016-2023 TRD Off-Road Auto (3rd gen) with V6
