## Aspect Ratio Guide

**Aspect Ratio** = (Sidewall Height / Section Width) × 100

### Common Ratios

| Aspect Ratio | Sidewall | Off-Road Use | Notes |
|--------------|----------|--------------|-------|
| 80-85 | Tall | Excellent | Maximum sidewall protection |
| 75 | Tall | Excellent | Great for rocks, flexes well |
| 70 | Medium | Good | Balanced road/trail |
| 65 | Medium | Fair | More road-oriented |
| 60 | Low | Poor | Vulnerable to sidewall damage |
| 55 | Low | Not Recommended | Street performance only |

**For Serious Off-Road**: Stick to 70+ aspect ratio
**For Rock Crawling**: 75+ is ideal
**For Overlanding**: 70-75 balances comfort and durability

## Load Range Guide

| Load Range | Ply Rating | Max PSI | Use Case |
|------------|------------|---------|----------|
| C | 6-ply | 50 PSI | Light use, better ride |
| D | 8-ply | 65 PSI | Medium-duty, overlanding |

**For Overlanding**: Load Range E for heavy builds
**For Daily Driver**: Load Range C or D for comfort
**For Rock Crawling**: D or E for sidewall strength

## Width Considerations

### Narrow (9.5" - 10.5")
- **Pros**: Better in mud, rocks, snow
- **Pros**: Less stress on IFS components
- **Pros**: Easier to fit without trimming
- **Cons**: Less flotation in sand

### Medium (11" - 12")
- **Pros**: Balanced performance
- **Pros**: Good all-around choice
- **Cons**: May require minor trimming

### Wide (12.5" - 14.5")
- **Pros**: Maximum flotation (sand)
- **Pros**: Larger contact patch
- **Cons**: Fender clearance issues
- **Cons**: Heavy steering load
- **Cons**: IFS CV angle problems

**IFS Vehicles**: Keep width ≤11.5" to avoid CV issues
**Solid Axle**: Width is less critical, 12.5" common

---

## Wheel Diameter Considerations

### 15" Wheels
- **Pros**: Maximum sidewall, best for off-road
- **Pros**: Cheapest tire options
- **Cons**: Limited modern fitment
- **Cons**: Brake clearance issues on newer trucks

### 16" Wheels
- **Pros**: Great sidewall, good tire selection
- **Pros**: Fits most modern trucks
- **Ideal For**: Tacoma, 4Runner, older Wranglers

### 17" Wheels
- **Pros**: Most popular size, huge selection
- **Pros**: Fits all modern vehicles
- **Ideal For**: Everything - most versatile

### 18"+ Wheels
- **Cons**: Less sidewall protection
- **Cons**: Harsher ride off-road
- **Use Case**: Street-focused builds only

**Recommendation**: 17" is the sweet spot for modern builds

## Decision Matrix

### Daily Driver + Weekend Trail
- **Size**: 285/70R17 or 285/75R16
- **Type**: All-Terrain (AT)
- **Load Range**: C or D
- **Example**: Falken AT3W, BFG KO2

### Serious Rock Crawling
- **Size**: 35x12.50R17 or larger
- **Type**: Mud-Terrain (MT)
- **Load Range**: E
- **Example**: BFG KM3, Goodyear MTR

### Overlanding / Expedition
- **Size**: 285/75R16 or LT285/70R17
- **Type**: All-Terrain (AT) or Hybrid (RT)
- **Load Range**: E (for heavy loads)
- **Example**: BFG KO2, Nitto Ridge Grappler

### Budget Build
- **Size**: 265/70R17 or 275/70R17
- **Type**: All-Terrain (AT)
- **Load Range**: C
- **Example**: Cooper AT3, Falken AT3W

## Toyota Tacoma Gear Ratio Guide

Understanding Tacoma gear ratios helps you choose the right tire size without needing expensive re-gearing.

### Factory Gear Ratios by Model Year

| Gear Ratio | Transmission | Model/Year | Best Tire Size | Notes |
|------------|--------------|------------|----------------|-------|
| **3.73** | 6MT, 6AT (early) | 2016-2019 SR5, Off-Road | 265/70R17 stock<br>285/70R17 upgrade | Economy-focused, feels underpowered with larger tires |
| **3.909** | 6AT | 2016-2023 TRD Off-Road (3rd gen)<br>2024+ Select packages (4th gen) | 265/70R17 stock<br>**285/75R17 ideal** | **Sweet spot ratio**<br>Best for overland builds |
| **4.10** | Various | TRD Pro, some Off-Road | 265/70R17 stock<br>285/75R17 comfortable<br>33x10.50R17 max | Strong acceleration, handles 33" well |
| **4.30** | 6AT (rare) | Special packages | 285/75R17 or larger | Excellent for heavy loads, ideal for 35" tires |

### Why 3.909 is the Hidden Gem

The **3.909 gear ratio** (found in 2016+ TRD Off-Road Auto, 3rd gen) is Toyota's best-kept secret:

**Advantages**:
- ✅ Stronger acceleration than 3.73 (especially when loaded)
- ✅ Better fuel economy than 4.10 (lower highway RPM)
- ✅ Handles 285/75R17 (33") without re-gearing
- ✅ Perfect for overlanding with gear + roof tent
- ✅ Maintains good crawl ratio (35.4:1 stock)

**Ideal Tire Sizes**:
- **Stock**: 265/70R17 (31.6")
- **Upgrade**: 285/75R17 (32.8") - **No re-gear needed**
- **Max without regear**: 285/75R17
- **With re-gear to 4.30**: 35x12.50R17

**Comparison at 65 mph** (with 285/75R17):
| Gear Ratio | RPM @ 65 mph | Performance | Fuel Economy | Re-Gear Needed? |
|------------|--------------|-------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 3.73 | 1,820 | Sluggish | Best | Yes (to 4.10) |
| **3.909** | **1,888** | **Good** | **Very Good** | **No** |
| 4.10 | 1,980 | Better | Good | No |
| 4.30 | 2,077 | Best | Fair | No (overkill) |

### Tacoma Tire Upgrade Paths

#### Path 1: Budget (3.73 Gears)
```
Stock 265/70R17 → 275/70R17 (no regear)
├─ Minimal diameter increase
├─ Fits stock suspension
└─ Keep 3.73 gears

OR

Stock 265/70R17 → 285/75R17 (regear to 4.10)
├─ Better tire size
├─ Requires 2" lift
└─ Budget $1,500 for gears
```

#### Path 2: Sweet Spot (3.909 Gears) - RECOMMENDED
```
Stock 265/70R17 → 285/75R17 (NO regear!)
├─ Perfect balance
├─ 2" lift required
├─ No regearing needed
└─ Best value upgrade

Optional future:
285/75R17 → 35x12.50R17 (regear to 4.56)
├─ For serious builds
├─ 3-4" lift required
└─ Budget $2,500 for gears
```

#### Path 3: Performance (4.10 Gears)
```
Stock 265/70R17 → 285/75R17 (no regear)
├─ Handles easily
├─ Good acceleration maintained
└─ Can push to 33x10.50R17

OR

Stock 265/70R17 → 35x12.50R17 (regear to 4.88)
├─ Serious build
├─ 3-4" lift required
└─ Re-gear mandatory
```

### Quick Decision Guide

**I have 3.73 gears, want 285/75R17**:
- Re-gear to 4.10? **Recommended** (noticeable improvement)
- Can I skip regear? **Yes, but sluggish when loaded**

**I have 3.909 gears, want 285/75R17**:
- Re-gear needed? **NO** (this is the perfect combo)
- Future upgrade path? 35" tires with 4.56 gears

**I have 4.10 gears, want 285/75R17**:
- Re-gear needed? **NO** (handles beautifully)
- Max tire size without regear? 33x10.50R17

**I have 4.10 gears, want 35x12.50R17**:
- Re-gear to 4.88? **Strongly recommended**
- Can I skip regear? **Not for daily driving**

## Pro Tips

1. **Buy 5 Matching Tires** - Always include a full-size spare
2. **Rotate Frequently** - Include spare in rotation (every 5k miles)
3. **Check Date Codes** - Don't buy tires older than 1 year
4. **Inflate Properly** - Higher PSI for highway, lower for trail
5. **Balance Matters** - Off-road tires need quality balancing
6. **Break-In Period** - 500 miles before judging noise/ride

## Tire Size Calculator Quick Reference

### Formula Cheat Sheet

**Metric Tire Diameter (inches)**:
```
((Width in mm × Aspect Ratio × 2) / 100 / 25.4) + Wheel Diameter
```

**Example: 285/75R16**
```
((285 × 75 × 2) / 100 / 25.4) + 16 = 32.83"
```

**Quick Estimate (±0.5")**:
```
Diameter ≈ Wheel + (Width × Aspect Ratio / 1270)
```

**Example: 285/75R16**
```
16 + (285 × 75 / 1270) ≈ 16 + 16.8 = 32.8"
```
