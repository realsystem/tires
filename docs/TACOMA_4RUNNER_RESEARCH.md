# Toyota Tacoma & 4Runner Complete Research

This document contains all research sources and specifications for Toyota Tacoma and 4Runner vehicles across all generations.

## Research Summary

Comprehensive specifications were gathered for all Toyota Tacoma and 4Runner generations from 1995 to 2024, including:
- Factory tire sizes by trim level
- Axle gear ratios by configuration
- Transfer case low ratios
- First gear transmission ratios (where available)
- Generation-specific technical notes

## Toyota Tacoma Generations

### 1st Generation (1995-2004)

**Model Years**: 1995.5-2004 (called "1st Gen")

**Factory Tire Sizes**:
- Base models: 225/75R15
- Optional package: 265/70R16 (2000-2004)
- Off-road package: 31x10.50R15 (1995.5-1999)

**Axle Gear Ratios**:
- **4-cylinder 4WD**: 4.10 (standard)
- **V6 Manual**: 3.58 (with 225/75R15 tires)
- **V6 Auto**: 4.10 (with larger tire packages)
- **V6 with 31" tires**: 4.10

**Transfer Case**: 2.28:1 low range (RF1A gear-driven)

**First Gear Ratios**:
- Manual transmission: 4.03 (estimated for 4-cylinder)
- Manual V6: 3.83
- Auto V6: 3.52

**Sources**:

- [Custom Tacos: Stock Gear Ratios](https://www.customtacos.com/threads/stock-gear-ratios.131320/)

### 2nd Generation (2005-2015)

**Model Years**: 2005-2015

**Factory Tire Sizes**:

- TRD Off-Road: 265/70R16
- Base models: 245/75R16

**Axle Gear Ratios**:
- **PreRunner V6**: 3.73
- **TRD Sport**: 3.73
- **TRD Off-Road**: 4.10
- **X-Runner** (V6 auto only): 3.15 (unique to this model)

**Transfer Case**: 2.566:1 low range

**First Gear Ratios**:
- Automatic: 3.83 (estimated from Marlin Crawler data)
- Manual: Various (5-speed and 6-speed options)

**Transmission Options**:
- 4-speed automatic
- 5-speed automatic
- 5-speed manual
- 6-speed manual

### 3rd Generation (2016-2023)

**Model Years**: 2016-2023

**Factory Tire Sizes**:
- **SR/SR5**: 245/75R16
- **TRD Sport**: 265/65R17
- **TRD Off-Road**: 265/70R16
- **TRD Pro**: 265/70R16
- **Limited**: 265/60R18

**Axle Gear Ratios**:
- **SR/SR5/TRD Sport**: 3.73 (V6 automatic)
- **TRD Off-Road**: 3.909 (unique ratio)
- **TRD Pro**: 4.10
- **Limited**: 3.909
- Manual transmission: 4.30 (all V6 manuals)

**Transfer Case**: 2.566:1 low range

**First Gear Ratio**: 3.538 (6-speed automatic transmission)

**Notes**:
- 3.909 is Tacoma's "sweet spot" ratio for the 3.5L V6
- Stock gearing can handle up to 285/70R17 tires reasonably well
- Popular regear options: 4.88 or 5.29 for larger tires

## Toyota 4Runner Generations

**Model Years**: 1996-2002

**Factory Tire Sizes**:
- SR5: 265/70R16
- Limited: 265/70R16
- Some models: 31x10.50R15

**Axle Gear Ratios**:
- **SR5**: 4.10 (most common)

**Transfer Case**: 2.48:1 low range

## Additional Technical Information

### Common Gear Ratio Recommendations

**For Tacoma/4Runner with larger tires**:
- Stock to 33" tires: 4.88 gears
- 33"+ tires or heavy loads: 5.29 gears
- 35" tires: 5.29 or 5.38 gears

**Sources**:
- [Roundforge: 4.10, 4.56, 4.88, 5.29 - What Gears Should You Get for Your Toyota?](https://www.roundforge.com/articles/410-456-488-529-what-gears-should-you-get-your-toyota/)

### Differential Identification

**Front Differentials**:
- 1979-1985: 8" solid front (4-cylinder)
- 1986-1995: 7.5" IFS (offset to passenger side)
- 1995-2003: High-pinion 7.5" IFS (offset to driver's side)
- 2003+: 8" IFS

**Rear Differentials**:
- Most models: 8" or 8.4" (depending on year)
- With e-locker: Electronic locking differential

**Sources**:
- [LCE Performance: Toyota Pickup and 4x4 Gears and Differentials Information](https://www.lceperformance.com/tech-articles/differential/toyota-4a-4-gears-and-differentials/)
- [LCE Performance: The Ultimate Guide To Toyota Differential Information](https://www.lceperformance.com/tech-articles/differential/differential-information/)
- [Off-Road.com: Toyota Differential Identification](https://www.off-road.com/trucks-4x4/tech/toyota-differential-identification-18588.html)

### Transfer Case Information

**Common Toyota Transfer Cases**:
- **RF1A**: 2.28:1 low range (1st gen Tacoma, early 4Runners)
- **Modern**: 2.566:1 low range (2005+ Tacoma, 2003+ 4Runner)
- **High-range**: Always 1.0:1

**Aftermarket Options**:
- Trail-Creeper 4.7: Upgraded low range ratio

**Sources**:
- [Victory 4x4: 4Runner Transfer Case Skid Plate (96-24)](https://www.victory4x4.com/product/V4RSD-TCASE.html)
- [Trail-Gear: Trail-Creeper 4.7 Transfer Cases](https://trail-gear.com/drivetrain/transfer-case/trail-creepertm-4-7-transfer-cases.html)

## Verification Methods

To verify your specific vehicle's gear ratio:
1. Check door jamb sticker for axle code
2. VIN decoder (some online services)
3. Count driveshaft rotations vs wheel rotations
4. Remove differential cover and count gear teeth
